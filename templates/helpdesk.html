❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)
How do I file a complaint? ▼
Log in and go to File Complaint from the navigation menu or dashboard. Choose the correct category, describe the issue clearly with your consumer number and relevant details. You will receive a unique Complaint ID upon successful submission. You can also attach supporting documents such as photos or bill copies.
Can a submitted complaint be edited? ▼
Yes, but only while the complaint is in Pending status. Once review begins, the complaint is locked to preserve the official record. You can however add follow-up information through the reply/conversation section on the complaint detail page.
What documents should I attach for billing issues? ▼
Upload your latest bill copy, a meter photo if available, and a brief note describing the discrepancy. Accepted formats are JPG, JPEG, PNG, and PDF. Maximum file size is 5 MB.
When should the Safety Hazard category be selected? ▼
Use it for exposed wires, sparking equipment, fallen lines, or any situation posing immediate danger to people or property. For life-threatening emergencies, call 1912 immediately rather than waiting for an online response.
How long does it take to resolve a complaint? ▼
Resolution times vary by complaint type:
  • Power Outage / Safety Hazard: 24–48 hours
  • Meter Problem: 3–5 working days
  • Billing Issue: 5–7 working days
  • Connection Request: 7–15 working days
  • General Query: 5–10 working days
How do I check the status of my complaint? ▼
Use the Track Complaint section above on this page. Alternatively go to My Complaints in the navigation menu to see all your complaints and their current statuses.
What if I forgot my Consumer ID? ▼
Your Consumer ID is shown on your Profile page after logging in. It follows the format CNSMR followed by 6 digits (e.g. CNSMR000001).
⚠ Safety & Outage Guidance
⚠  For life-threatening electrical emergencies, call 1912 immediately. Do not wait.
#Safety Guideline
1 Never touch broken, hanging, or fallen power lines. Keep bystanders at a safe distance and call 1912 immediately. Do not attempt to move or repair lines yourself.
2 Switch off appliances and unplug sensitive electronics during voltage fluctuations or storm-related outages to prevent damage from sudden power surges when supply resumes.
3 Dial 1912 for urgent electricity failure and hazard reporting. This helpline is available 24×7, toll-free. Keep your Consumer ID ready for faster service.
4 Keep your Complaint ID and Consumer Number ready when contacting support for faster identification and resolution.
5 Do not tamper with electricity meters or attempt unauthorised connections. This is a punishable offence under the Electricity Act and poses serious safety risks.
